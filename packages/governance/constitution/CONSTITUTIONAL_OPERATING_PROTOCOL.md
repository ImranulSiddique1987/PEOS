# Chapter 1 — Constitutional Foundation

## 1.1 Purpose

The Constitutional Operating Protocol (COP) establishes the mandatory operational rules governing engineering activities, implementation practices, architectural decision making, documentation behavior, repository management, AI collaboration, validation processes, and execution workflows throughout the PEOS ecosystem.

This document defines _how_ PEOS shall be implemented while preserving constitutional compliance at every stage of development.

---

## 1.2 Constitutional Status

This document is classified as a Constitutional Governance Specification.

Its provisions are mandatory unless explicitly superseded through a constitutional amendment approved under the governance process defined by PEOS.

No implementation activity may intentionally violate this protocol.

---

## 1.3 Constitutional Authority

COP derives its authority from the PEOS Constitution.

It does not replace the Constitution.

It operationalizes the Constitution.

Whenever constitutional interpretation is required, the Constitution shall prevail.

---

## 1.4 Relationship to PEOS Constitution

The Constitution defines governing philosophy.

COP defines operational execution.

The Constitution answers:

> What PEOS is.

COP answers:

> How PEOS is operated.

---

## 1.5 Relationship to PMIR

PMIR defines implementation milestones.

COP defines implementation behavior.

PMIR answers:

> What must be implemented.

COP answers:

> How implementation shall be executed.

---

## 1.6 Relationship to PAS

PAS governs roadmap synchronization.

COP governs engineering execution.

PAS controls milestone progression.

COP controls implementation conduct.

---

## 1.7 Relationship to ADRs

Architecture Decision Records document architectural decisions.

COP governs the decision-making process leading to those records.

ADRs are evidence.

COP is policy.

---

## 1.8 Relationship to Repository

The repository is the executable realization of constitutional governance.

Repository contents shall remain consistent with:

1. Constitution
2. PMIR
3. PAS
4. COP
5. Approved ADRs

---

## 1.9 Constitutional Applicability

COP applies to:

- Human contributors
- AI assistants
- Automation systems
- Build pipelines
- Code generators
- Documentation generators
- Runtime implementations
- Future engineering tools

No contributor is exempt.

---

## 1.10 Amendment Policy

COP may only be amended through constitutional governance.

Operational convenience shall never justify constitutional modification.

All amendments shall preserve backward governance compatibility whenever reasonably achievable.
